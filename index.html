Future

Where I want my work to go

Not a rigid plan, but a direction: business-aware data science that stays human and honest about its impact.

Business-aware data science

I want to deepen my foundations in machine learning, statistics, time-series analysis and data engineering and apply them to problems where the business constraints are as real as the math.

My ideal work sits at the overlap of ML models, product thinking, and operations — where better decisions, not just better dashboards, are the outcome.

AI that remembers people, not just metrics

My social work keeps me grounded in what numbers can’t see: context, dignity, and access. I want my future research and work to stay anchored in inclusion and impact — especially for small businesses and underserved communities.

Whether it’s pricing, behaviour through sensor data, or outreach, my goal is simple: use data to expand opportunity, not just efficiency.

Thinking

How I like to think

I’m drawn to methods that respect the messiness of human behaviour and still produce clear, honest insights.

Areas I’m curious about

  • Time-series & sensor data – especially from wearables and real-world devices.
  • Experiment design & causal reasoning around pricing, interventions and behaviour.
  • Responsible & inclusive AI – how models interact with people at the margins.

My problem-solving style

I like to structure ambiguous problems into clear questions, identify what can and cannot be measured, and then choose simple, robust methods before fancy ones.

Most of my decisions start with a small scratchpad: assumptions, constraints, people involved, and failure modes. Only then do I reach for tools, models, or slides.
